CREATING AN ACCOUNT

This guide will already assume you have downloaded WiA and that you have logged onto an anonymous account. If you need assistance with either of these things then you should head over to the Whispers In Akarra official website and click on the FAQ link under Support.

To get an account, you need to have an admin create one for you. There are a few ways to do this:

1. Log onto an anonymous account in WiA and ask an admin to create one for you. To find an administrator simply press F8 or click the People menu. Administrators have a ¤ beside their name.

2. If you have IRC, log onto the Quakenet server, join the #Akarra channel, and ask for an account.

3. Click on Contact link under Support at the Whispers In Akarra official website and email the appropriate person.

When asking for an account, be sure to include the account name and password you wish to use and your current valid email address.

After your account has been created open WiA, create a new character, and log into the game. For detailed information about deities and character classes please click here.

NAVIGATING THE MENU SCREENS

Clicking on a menu button will display information in the menu screen. Below is a list of the menu buttons and the information they contain.

You can press F1 at any time to display the Help menu.

Name: Displays your name in WiA.
Mundane Level 0: All characters start as Mundane until you acquire the skills to advance to different classes. Level displays your current experience level.
Color of the Deity: - Displays your soul color and religion.

Experience: Displays your total amount of experience points.
Next Level: You will gain a level when you reach the amount of experience displayed here.
Next Attribute: You will gain an attribute point when you reach the amount of experience displayed here.
Attribute Points: Displays the current amount of attribute points which you may assign to any of the attributes (listed below). To assign an attribute point, click the "Use Attribute Point" button and select the attribute of your choice.

ATTRIBUTES

Strength: Your physical power. It allows you to wield heavier weapons and wear heavier armor. It also increases the amount of damage you inflict in melee combat.
Constitution: Your physical health. It increases your health points and stamina points. It also increases your resistance towards red-, blue-, and yellow-based spells.
Agility: Describes your nimbleness and dexterity. High agility makes it easier to hit targets as well as to avoid enemy blows. Agility is also a requirement for certain items.
Intelligence: The power of your mind. It makes your spells more effective and protects you against green-based spells. Intelligence is also an important item requirement.
Faith: How well you work with divine powers. Faith increases your magic points and your resistance against white-based spells. Faith is also very important for items.

Health Points: Displays your current amount / total amount of health.
Magic Points: Displays your current amount / total amount of magic.
Stamina Points: Displays your current amount / total amount of stamina.

ABILITIES

Attack Rating: Determines your ability to successfully hit during combat (especially monsters and players with a high armor class).
Cast Rating: Magic does not yet exist in WiA
Minimum Damage: The minimum physical damage you can do in combat.
Maximum Damage: The maximum physical damage you can do in combat.
Armor Class: Determines your ability to successfully dodge blows during combat.
Resist White: Resistance to white-based spells.
Resist Red: Resistance to red-based spells.
Resist Green: Resistance to green-based spells.
Resist Blue: Resistance to blue-based spells.
Resist Yellow: Resistance to yellow-based spells.

EQUIPMENT

The equipment slots displays any weapons, armor, and items you are currently wearing. To equip an item, simply drag it from the backpack and onto the equipment slots. It doesn't matter which slot you drop it in because the item will automatically be placed into the correct slot (if equippable).

BACKPACK

Your backpack holds all of your weapons, armor, items, food, etc. For every 10 levels of experience you achieve, you will gain 1 extra row of 5 empty slots. The maximum amount of rows you can acquire is 6 (30 empty slots total).

MONEY

Coins: Displays the amount of coins you have / the total amount of coins you can hold. Coins are divided into 3 currencies: copper coins (cc), silver coins (sc), gold coins (gc).

100cc = 1sc
10sc = 1gc

Do not worry about converting your coins. The game will automatically do it for you.

SKILLS AND SPELLS

Displays all of the skills you have learned. To learn a skill you must find a Skill Scroll and read it. To read a scroll simply drag it from your backpack to your equipment slots (or you may "use" the scroll on yourself in the same manner).

UNASSIGNED PROFICIENCY GEMS

Each gem increases the power of a skill by one step. A skill can have up to 5 gems, one of each color. You receive new gems as you progress levels (1 gem every level and 2 gems every five levels).

QUESTS

This menu keeps track of all your quests, their objectives, and the ones you have finished. To obtain a quest talk to a NPC. Some quests will only be given to you if you meet certain criteria.

GAME MAPS

View Worldmap: Click this button to view the map of the entire world.
View Sectormap: Click this button to view the map of the sector you are in.

GAME OPTIONS

Help!: Click this button to display the Help menu.
Set Chat Prefix: Click this button to set a chat prefix. Setting a chat prefix will automatically enter the prefix when you type in the chat interface.
Message Settings: Click this button to configure which types of messages will (and will not) be displayed or where they should be displayed.
Draw Player Names: Checking this box will display the names of the players above their heads.

Show Experience Drop-off: Checking this box will display how much experience was dropped off after killing a monster. To learn more about experience drop-offs please click here.

SYSTEM OPTIONS

Play Sound Effects: Checking this box will turn on the sound effects.

EXPLORING THE WORLD

If you're not sure what to do, please read the short summary below. I'm not going to go into full details on this, but it should be enough to get you started.

You begin in the city of Hope. Your first objective should be to talk to Nicholas the Elder. He will provide you with some important information so listen very carefully to what he says. He also provides you with your first quest.

After speaking with Nicholas, head south-southwest on the trail as he has instructed and you will find Rupert the Farmer. Rupert also gives you a quest.

At this point you should fight rabbits and rats to gain a few levels. After you have completed Rupert's quest, the world is yours for the exploration. Head north onto the peninsula and explore everywhere. If you meet a few other players on your journey, it would be a good idea to form a group and create a fellowship. Fellowships will help you to grow strong quickly and they are a key to survival.
